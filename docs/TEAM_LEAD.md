## 🎯 Weekly Show & Tell Format (30-45 min)

**Purpose:** Celebrate progress, share learnings, build community

**Format:**
1. **Quick Wins** (10 min)
   - Each person shares one thing they learned or built
   - Keep it brief (1-2 minutes each)
   - Encourage applause/emojis

2. **Demo Time** (15-20 min)
   - 2-3 people demo their weekly project
   - Rotate who presents each week
   - Ask questions, give positive feedback

3. **Problem Solving** (10 min)
   - Someone shares a bug they're stuck on
   - Group helps brainstorm solutions
   - Learn debugging together

4. **Next Week Preview** (5 min)
   - Tease what's coming
   - Build excitement
   - Remind of support available

**Tips:**
- Keep it light and fun
- No criticism - only constructive feedback
- Celebrate attempts, not just success
- Make it safe to share struggles

Legend:
- ✅ Complete
- 🟡 In progress  
- ⬜ Not started

### Check-in Questions
Weekly 1-on-1 or survey:
1. Did you complete this week's exercises?
2. What was most challenging?
3. What was most exciting?
4. Do you need any support?
5. Estimated hours spent this week?

### Red Flags to Watch For
- ⚠️ Missing 2+ weekly check-ins
- ⚠️ Not committing code to GitHub
- ⚠️ Asking no questions (might be stuck but not speaking up)
- ⚠️ Expressing frustration or wanting to quit
- ⚠️ Not attending show & tell

**Action:** Reach out personally, offer 1-on-1 support

## ⚠️ Common Challenges & Solutions

### Challenge: Students falling behind
**Solution:**
- Offer catch-up sessions
- Extend timeline if needed
- Pair with buddy more actively
- Reduce daily time expectation, extend program duration

### Challenge: Copilot not working
**Solution:**
- Verify license allocation
- Check VS Code settings
- Update extensions
- Have backup AI tool ready (ChatGPT for explanation)

### Challenge: Low engagement
**Solution:**
- Make show & tell more fun (prizes, games)
- Connect more to real work projects
- Check if time commitment is realistic
- Get management buy-in for dedicated learning time

### Challenge: Wide skill variation
**Solution:**
- Let advanced students help others (teaching reinforces learning)
- Provide extension challenges for fast learners
- Offer 1-on-1 support for struggling students
- Emphasize personal progress over comparison

### Challenge: Imposter syndrome
**Solution:**
- Normalize struggle ("everyone finds this hard at first")
- Share your own learning struggles
- Celebrate small wins publicly
- Create safe space for questions
